An attacker can exploit some of these vulnerabilities to take control of an affected system. CVE-2022-30190, also known as “Follina” — the RCE vulnerability in the Microsoft Windows Support Diagnostic Tool that was disclosed in late May and exploited in the wild — has now received patches for affected Windows systems. While Microsoft had provided mitigation guidance in an advisory on May 30, patches were not released until June 14.
