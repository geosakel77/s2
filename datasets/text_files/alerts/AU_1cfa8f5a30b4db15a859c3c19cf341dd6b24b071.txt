Alert status HIGH Security researchers have developed methods involving speculative execution to read kernel memory from user space on a variety of processors from a range of vendors produced in the last decade. These methods have been referred to as Meltdown and Spectre. Meltdown is described as a vulnerability that allows a program to access the memory, and thus also the secrets, of other programs and the operating system. Meltdown only impacts Intel chips. Spectre allows access to protected memory of other applications. Spectre impacts Intel, AMD and ARM chips, which includes some mobile devices. 29 Jan 2020 Revised patch released to disable mitigation against Spectre variant 2 11 Jan 2020 Meltdown and Spectre patches unsuitable for some security products 09 Jan 2019 Advice remains that organisations should patch Meltdown/Spectre vulnerabilities 05 Jan 2019 Patch your devices for Meltdown and Spectre vulnerabilities as soon as possible Content written for Individuals & families Small & medium businesses Organisations & critical infrastructure Government View all content First published: 05 Jan 2018 Last updated: 29 Jan 2020 Share on Was this information helpful?
